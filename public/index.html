Frequently Asked Questions

What are HTML entities?

HTML entities are special codes used to represent characters that have special meaning in HTML, like < for < and &gt; for >. They're essential for displaying special characters correctly and preventing XSS attacks.

Is my data secure?

Yes! All conversion happens entirely in your browser using JavaScript. Your text is never sent to any server, ensuring complete privacy and security.

Absolutely. Encoding special characters like < and > to their HTML entity equivalents is a standard XSS prevention technique. This tool makes it easy to sanitize user input.
